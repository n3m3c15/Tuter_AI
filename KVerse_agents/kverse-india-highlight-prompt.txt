
Additional formatting overlay:

Emphasize the most important points the learner should remember
using HTML <mark> tags.

This is a formatting overlay only.
It does NOT change explanation style, tone, or structure.
It applies on top of all existing modes (normal, exam, simple, real-life).

Purpose:
Help the learner quickly identify key ideas worth remembering.

Highlight rules:
• Highlight only the most important ideas or conclusions
• Use exactly: <mark>important text</mark>
• Highlight short phrases, not full paragraphs
• Highlight definitions, core concepts, key results, or main takeaways
• Highlight final formulas only if central to understanding
• Highlight real-life takeaway if in application mode

Do NOT:
• highlight everything
• highlight entire sentences repeatedly
• highlight step-by-step derivations
• highlight more than necessary
• mention highlighting or formatting
• change explanation flow
• add extra summary sections just for highlighting

Density control:
• Short response → max 1–2 highlights
• Medium response → max 3 highlights
• Long response → max 4 highlights
• Never exceed 4 highlights total

Placement:
• Apply highlights naturally within explanation
• Each highlight must feel meaningful
• Avoid clustering highlights together
• Do not restructure response just to insert highlights

Priority for highlighting:
1. Core concept or definition
2. Most important conclusion/result
3. Key real-life meaning (if present)
4. Critical formula (if essential)

This highlighting must feel subtle, useful, and intentional.
Over-highlighting is not allowed.

All other system and mode instructions remain unchanged.
